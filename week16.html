Matchup Summaries

Luka my Balls 1455.95 def. Big Nik Energy 1270.75

Luka my Balls (13-3): 44 games, 33.09 fppg (G: 31.7 | F: 34.6 | C: 33.9)

Big Nik Energy (4-12): 37 games, 34.34 fppg (G: 32.0 | F: 31.6 | C: 41.3)

Season series: Nick 5-1 | All-time: Nick leads 36-27

Nick came in as a -510 moneyline favorite with a 277.5-point spread, and while he took care of business, the win didn't feel nearly as dominant as the line suggested. The 185.20-point margin was comfortable enough, but well short of what the spread demanded -- a rare instance of Nick winning outright while failing to cover.

The storyline belonged to Nick's guards, who outscored Hayden's by 250 FP on the week. Jalen Johnson led the charge with 182.35 FP across just 3 games, backed by Anthony Edwards (166.75 FP, 4 games) delivering the consistent production Nick has come to expect. Payton Pritchard (137.20 FP, 4 games) rounded out a devastating backcourt attack that Hayden simply couldn't match. On Hayden's side, Nikola Jokic did everything he could -- his 192.65 FP across 3 games was the week's best individual performance -- but the loss of 11 games to injury across his roster created too deep a hole to climb out of.

The victory extends Nick's win streak to 7, establishing a new season-best mark and leaving him just one win shy of tying his personal all-time record of 8 consecutive victories, set back in 2020-21. More importantly, the magic number drops to 2: any combination of one Nick win and one Benton loss clinches the regular season crown. With title odds now at 96.0% and Hayden mathematically eliminated from contention, the three-peat is all but locked in. Nick is now 5-1 against Hayden on the season and holds a commanding 36-27 edge in the all-time series.

Saboner 1581.35 def. Smaxey 1389.50

Saboner (6-10): 41 games, 38.57 fppg (G: 37.6 | F: 34.8 | C: 44.9)

Smaxey (9-7): 39 games, 35.63 fppg (G: 41.1 | F: 35.9 | C: 30.4)

Season series: Benton 4-2 | All-time: Garrett leads 32-31

Garrett snapped a 3-game losing streak by covering the 71.0-point spread with authority, rolling to a 191.85-point victory that tilted the all-time series in his favor for the first time all season. The margin told the story: Garrett was the -150 moneyline favorite, but nobody expected this kind of separation.

Kawhi Leonard delivered the performance of his season -- 191.00 FP across 4 games -- proving that when healthy, he's still one of the most complete fantasy assets in the league. Jalen Brunson (159.95 FP, 4 games) and Jamal Murray (147.90 FP, 3 games) provided the depth scoring that's been missing during Garrett's recent slide. The 104.0% efficiency rating marked a welcome return to form, with his centers absolutely dominating at 44.9 FPPG to Benton's 30.4 -- a 14.5-point positional advantage that swung the matchup.

Benton's camp got contributions from Tyrese Maxey (184.00 FP, 4 games) and Trey Murphy III (165.65 FP, 3 games), but 8 games lost to injury left the roster running short. The loss drops Benton to 9-7 and deals a significant blow to whatever championship hopes remained -- his title odds crater from 11.5% to just 4.0%. Meanwhile, Garrett's win solidifies his hold on third place at 97.70% probability, all but ensuring he avoids the basement. The two teams are now deadlocked at 32-31 all-time, the league's most evenly contested rivalry.

Player of the Week

Nikola Jokic

192.65 FP over 3 games (64.22 FPPG) | +7.55% vs projection | 15.16% of team output

The big Serbian reminded everyone why he's the best fantasy asset in basketball. Three games, three triple-double caliber performances, and an 84.1-point explosion against Chicago on February 7th that stood as the week's single-best game by any player in the league. That Chicago outing alone would have been a good week for most players; for Jokic, it was just Saturday.

This marks Jokic's fourth POTW award of the season -- tied for the most by any player -- after also winning in Weeks 3, 5, and 10. The 64.22 FPPG average was untouchable despite the limited sample size, and his 7.55% surplus over projection showed that even the lofty expectations built into his stat line understate what he's capable of when locked in. The only frustration for Hayden is that Jokic's dominance came during a week where the rest of the roster was decimated by injury and blunders. When your best player accounts for 15.16% of your team's output and you still lose by 185 points, you know the cavalry didn't show up.

The POTW race by manager now stands at Benton 5, Hayden 5, Nick 4, Garrett 2. Jokic has won a quarter of all weekly awards this season, cementing his status as the league's most consistently elite performer even as Hayden's season spirals toward the basement.

Honorable Mentions: Kawhi Leonard posted 191.00 FP across 4 games (47.75 FPPG), his best week since returning from injury and the performance that sparked Garrett's dominant victory. Tyrese Maxey delivered 184.00 FP across 4 games (46.00 FPPG) but couldn't carry Benton's shorthanded roster across the finish line.
